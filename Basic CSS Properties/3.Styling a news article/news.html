A powerful earthquake measuring 7.2 struck Southern California at 3:15 PM local time, with its epicenter located near Los Angeles. The tremor has caused extensive damage, particularly in downtown LA, where several buildings have been evacuated due to structural concerns. Emergency services are actively responding, assessing the damage, and providing assistance to those affected. Witnesses reported scenes of chaos as people rushed into the streets and neighbors helped each other. In response to the disaster, the Governor has declared a state of emergency, urging residents to remain indoors and avoid unnecessary travel. Authorities are warning that aftershocks are likely, and the public is advised to stay vigilant. Further updates will be provided as more information becomes available.
